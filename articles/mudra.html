Mudra Loan Scheme 2026

Published: 2026

Introduction

Mudra Loan Scheme, also known as Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY), is a government initiative launched to support small businesses, entrepreneurs and self-employed individuals in India. The scheme was introduced by the Government of India to provide financial assistance to people who want to start or expand small businesses but do not have access to traditional bank loans.

Small businesses play a crucial role in the Indian economy. Millions of people across the country depend on small enterprises, shops, manufacturing units and service businesses for their livelihood. However, many entrepreneurs face difficulties in obtaining loans because they lack collateral or proper financial history.

To solve this problem, the government introduced the Mudra Loan Scheme. Under this scheme, banks and financial institutions provide loans to small businesses without requiring collateral.

Overview of Mudra Loan Scheme

The Mudra Loan Scheme was launched in April 2015 as part of the government’s efforts to support micro and small enterprises.

  • Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana
  • Launch Year: 2015
  • Purpose: Support small businesses
  • Loan Provider: Banks and financial institutions

Types of Mudra Loans

Mudra loans are divided into three categories based on the loan amount and business stage.

  • Shishu Loan: Loans up to ₹50,000
  • Kishore Loan: Loans from ₹50,000 to ₹5,00,000
  • Tarun Loan: Loans from ₹5,00,000 to ₹10,00,000

Objectives of Mudra Loan Scheme

  • Promote entrepreneurship
  • Support micro and small enterprises
  • Create employment opportunities
  • Encourage economic growth
  • Provide financial support for small businesses

Eligibility Criteria

  • Applicant must be an Indian citizen
  • Applicant must run or plan to start a small business
  • Business must fall under micro or small enterprise category
  • Applicant must provide a business proposal

Documents Required

  • Aadhar Card
  • PAN Card
  • Address Proof
  • Bank Account Details
  • Business Plan
  • Passport Size Photograph

Benefits of Mudra Loan Scheme

  • Loans without collateral
  • Financial support for entrepreneurs
  • Support for small businesses
  • Encouragement for self-employment
  • Opportunity to expand business

How to Apply for Mudra Loan

  1. Visit the nearest bank branch.
  2. Request Mudra loan application form.
  3. Fill in business and personal details.
  4. Submit required documents.
  5. Bank will review and approve loan.

Industries Supported by Mudra Loans

  • Small manufacturing businesses
  • Retail shops
  • Transport services
  • Food processing units
  • Repair shops
  • Handicraft businesses

Impact of Mudra Loan Scheme

Since its launch, the Mudra Loan Scheme has helped millions of entrepreneurs start and expand businesses across India. The scheme has contributed significantly to job creation and economic growth.

Conclusion

Mudra Loan Scheme is a major initiative that supports small businesses and promotes entrepreneurship in India. By providing loans without collateral, the scheme helps individuals start and grow businesses that contribute to the country’s economic development.
