Mudra Loan Scheme 2026

Introduction

Mudra Loan Scheme, also known as Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY), is a government initiative launched to support small businesses, entrepreneurs and self-employed individuals in India. The scheme was introduced by the Government of India to provide financial assistance to people who want to start or expand small businesses but do not have access to traditional bank loans.

Small businesses play a crucial role in the Indian economy. Millions of people across the country depend on small enterprises, shops, manufacturing units and service businesses for their livelihood. However, many entrepreneurs face difficulties in obtaining loans because they lack collateral or proper financial history.

To solve this problem, the government introduced the Mudra Loan Scheme. Under this scheme, banks and financial institutions provide loans to small businesses without requiring collateral. The objective is to encourage entrepreneurship and promote economic growth.

Overview of Mudra Loan Scheme

The Mudra Loan Scheme was launched in April 2015 as part of the government’s efforts to support micro and small enterprises. The scheme provides loans to individuals involved in manufacturing, trading and service sectors.

Mudra loans are provided through banks, microfinance institutions and non-banking financial companies. The loan amount depends on the type and scale of the business.

  • Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana
  • Launch Year: 2015
  • Purpose: Support small businesses and entrepreneurs
  • Loan Provider: Banks and financial institutions

Types of Mudra Loans

Mudra loans are divided into three categories depending on the size of the business and loan amount required.

  • Shishu Loan: Loans up to ₹50,000 for small businesses starting operations.
  • Kishore Loan: Loans from ₹50,000 to ₹5,00,000 for growing businesses.
  • Tarun Loan: Loans from ₹5,00,000 to ₹10,00,000 for established businesses.

These categories help ensure that businesses at different stages of development can receive appropriate financial support.

Objectives of Mudra Loan Scheme

The Mudra Loan Scheme was introduced with several important objectives aimed at strengthening India's economy and supporting entrepreneurs.

  • Promote entrepreneurship and self-employment
  • Provide financial assistance to small businesses
  • Encourage micro and small enterprise development
  • Create employment opportunities
  • Support economic growth and development

Eligibility Criteria

Individuals and small business owners who wish to apply for a Mudra loan must meet certain eligibility requirements.

  • Applicant must be an Indian citizen
  • Applicant must be involved in a small business or entrepreneurial activity
  • Business must fall under the micro or small enterprise category
  • Applicant must provide a business plan or proposal

Mudra loans are commonly used by shop owners, artisans, manufacturers, service providers and small traders.

Documents Required

Applicants must submit certain documents while applying for a Mudra loan. These documents help financial institutions verify the applicant's identity and business information.

  • Aadhar Card
  • PAN Card
  • Bank Account Details
  • Business Plan
  • Address Proof
  • Passport Size Photograph

Benefits of Mudra Loan Scheme

The Mudra Loan Scheme provides several benefits to entrepreneurs and small business owners across India.

  • Loans available without collateral
  • Support for small and micro enterprises
  • Encouragement for new entrepreneurs
  • Financial assistance for business expansion
  • Employment generation opportunities

These benefits make Mudra loans an attractive option for individuals who want to start or grow a small business.

How to Apply for Mudra Loan

Applicants can apply for Mudra loans through banks, microfinance institutions and financial organizations that participate in the scheme.

  1. Visit a nearby bank or financial institution.
  2. Request the Mudra Loan application form.
  3. Fill out the form with required details.
  4. Submit necessary documents.
  5. Wait for verification and loan approval.

Once the loan is approved, the amount is transferred to the applicant's bank account.

Industries Supported by Mudra Loans

Mudra loans are available for a wide range of industries and businesses including:

  • Small manufacturing units
  • Retail shops
  • Food processing businesses
  • Transportation services
  • Repair shops
  • Handicrafts and artisan businesses

Impact of Mudra Loan Scheme

Since its launch, the Mudra Loan Scheme has helped millions of small businesses and entrepreneurs across India. Many individuals who previously lacked access to financial support have been able to start successful businesses.

The scheme has contributed to employment generation and has strengthened the micro and small enterprise sector in the country.

Conclusion

Mudra Loan Scheme is an important initiative that supports small businesses and promotes entrepreneurship in India. By providing loans without collateral, the scheme enables individuals to start and expand businesses that contribute to economic development.

Entrepreneurs who have innovative business ideas but lack financial resources can benefit greatly from the Mudra Loan Scheme. By taking advantage of this opportunity, individuals can build successful businesses and create employment opportunities for others.
